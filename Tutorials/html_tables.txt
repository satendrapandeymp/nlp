<header> Table Header, Body, and Footer </header>
<para> Tables can be divided into three portions − a header, a body, and a foot. The head and foot are rather similar to headers and footers in a word-processed document that remain the same for every page, while the body is the main content holder of the table. </para>
<para> The three elements for separating the head, body, and foot of a table are − </para>
<para> <thead> − to create a separate table header. </para>
<para> <tbody> − to indicate the main body of the table. </para>
<para> <tfoot> − to create a separate table footer. </para>
<para> A table may contain several <tbody> elements to indicate different pages or groups of data. But it is notable that <thead> and <tfoot> tags should appear before <tbody> </para>
